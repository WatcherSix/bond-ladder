Model Assumptions and Limits

Core Income Model

  • Coupon bonds pay annual coupon (face x rate) split evenly across configured coupon months.
  • Coupon cash flow is not pro-rated by day count, settlement date, accrued interest, or partial periods.
  • Zero-coupon income contribution is modeled as gain only (face - cost) in the maturity month.
  • No defaults, calls, reinvestment delays, tax effects, or spread/price volatility are modeled.

Full Ladder and Stage 2 Math

  • Full Ladder pairs months six months apart and sizes capital from annual pair gap / assumed yield.
  • Yield is treated as a single flat annual input, not a term structure by maturity.
  • Stage 2 recommendations size buckets based on selected frequency (monthly, annual, semiannual, or custom months).
  • Budget allocation is greedy by largest annual pair gap, not optimized with transaction constraints.
  • Scenario view shocks only yield (plus/minus bps) while holdings and target remain fixed.

Audit and Data Quality

  • Audit reconciliation validates internal model consistency, not brokerage statement truth.
  • Duplicate detection uses normalized bond fingerprints; near-identical rows can be treated as duplicates.
  • CSV import requires mappable face/coupon/maturity fields and sanitizes invalid or out-of-range entries.
  • Dates are normalized to ISO where possible; ambiguous source formats may still require manual review.

Stage 4 Reinvestment Simulator

  • Projection runs in yearly blocks over a fixed horizon with static target income.
  • Configured percentage of carry cash is deployed at the start of each projection year.
  • Reinvested face is modeled with simple monthly accrual at the fixed reinvestment yield.
  • Maturity proceeds become carry cash for future deployment; transaction costs and slippage are excluded.
  • Outputs are planning approximations for mechanics validation, not investment advice.
Assumptions are intentionally explicit so reviewers can verify mechanics and limitations before relying on outputs.
